## What Are Staff Reports?

Staff reports are documents prepared by City of Toronto staff that are submitted to City Council alongside agenda items. Staff reports are used to inform, advise, and support decision-making by City Council and its Committees. A report can be written by one division or by several. 

## What Are Staff Reports For?

After City staff research issues, consider policy options, and talk to stakeholders, they make recommendations to Council. These recommendations are often accompanied by a staff report, which provides a summary of the justification for the recommendations City staff have made. This can include a summary of the research conducted, and an explanation of the key issues, options and potential impacts. 

Staff reports help Council make decisions that are based on solid evidence and reasoning. Staff reports play an important role in demonstrating policy and legal alignment across the City. Staff reports also create a public record of how and why decisions are made. This helps promote transparency and accountability. 

## What is in a Staff Report?

The basic building blocks of a staff report are:

1. Summary  
2. Recommendations  
3. Financial Impact  
4. Decision History  
5. Comments  
6. Contact  
7. Signature  
8. Attachments

What is in each of these sections is elaborated below.

### Staff Report Headers

The formatting for staff reports is standardized across the City. At the very top left, we see the City of Toronto logo. At the top right, we see “REPORT FOR ACTION”. A Report for Action requests Council to approve recommendations. You may also see a Report for Information, which provides updates, data, or research, requiring no formal decision.

Next, we see the date that the report was completed (February 10, 2026), which committee the report was submitted to (Economic and Community Development Committee), who the report is coming from (generally the head of the division that wrote the report \- in this case, the General Manager, Economic Development and Culture), and which wards the report is relevant to. In this case, the report is relevant to all wards.

### Summary

The Summary is an overview of a staff report. It is a great thing to read if you are trying to get the gist of what a staff report is talking about. It can also be found on the agenda item page ([https://secure.toronto.ca/council/agenda-item.do?item=2026.EC26.2](https://secure.toronto.ca/council/agenda-item.do?item=2026.EC26.2)). This can make it easier to browse items and get a sense for what the staff report is about.

A Summary section is in every staff report. It will typically give an overview of the proposed recommendations and a brief rationale. A Summary should also be a roadmap for what one can expect from the rest of the report.

### Recommendations

The next section in the staff report is the Recommendations section. Please see the Recommendations Wiki (***to be added soon***) entry for more details. 

Recommendations are written by staff to be voted on by Council. Recommendations direct Council, staff, or divisions to do a variety of different actions.

For example, one can see above that the first recommendation is that City Council adopt Toronto’s Festival Strategy, as outlined in Attachment 1 to the report. If City Council chooses to adopt this recommendation, it would adopt the Strategy. This signals their endorsement of the Strategy and for the Division to move forward with the work in the Strategy. 

Recommendations can also set up new programs. In recommendation 2, staff have written a recommendation that directs the division that wrote the report (Economic Development and Culture) to establish a new funding program. The details of the funding program are found in Attachment 1 of the report.

### Financial Impact

If one of the recommendations has a budget implication (e.g. a new program will require a new budget, or as you see in this example, that the previous budgets will receive increases aligned with inflation), the budget implication will be outlined in the Financial Impact section. 

If there is no budget implication, this section may only say that the work will be able to continue with existing resources, or that there may be future budget requests that arise as the work continues. An example of this language can be seen in the top paragraph of this example. 

If there is a new budget ask associated with a staff report, the Financial Impact section will outline what the new budget ask will be. However, the actual budget approval will not be complete until the next budget cycle. This happens once a year.

### Decision History

The Decision History section of a staff report will summarize previous Council decisions that are relevant to the topic of the staff report. These can be decisions that are directly related to the staff report (e.g. at a previous Council meeting, Council directed staff to report back in a year with a Festival Strategy) or indirectly related, but still relevant (e.g. at a previous Council meeting, Council requested additional funding from the federal and provincial governments for festival safety and security). 

This is a great section to understand the context in which a staff report is being written. If you are also curious about the history of an issue or topic, the Decision History section is an informative section to check out.

### Comments

The Comments section is usually the largest section of a staff report. This gives Council an opportunity to understand the rationale and research behind the recommendations in a report. 

This section will often summarize the background on a topic, the context (e.g. see the subheading “The City’s Support for Festivals”), the research or work conducted to arrive at the outcome of the report (e.g. see the subheadings “Developing the Festival Strategy”, and “Key Findings from Research and Engagement”), relevant timelines, and plans for monitoring and evaluating new strategies and programs. 

There is also often a Next Steps section in a report at the end of the Comments section.  

### Contact

The contact listed in this section is the person who was responsible for supervising/working on/developing the staff report. They are often staff who work below the head of the division. They can be great people to get in touch with if you are interested in learning more about the report or the topic of the staff report. 

### Signature

Next, we have the Signature section, which will be the “signature” of the head of the division that wrote the report. In this case, it is Patrick Tobin, the General Manager of Economic Development and Culture.

### Attachments

Attachments are the last section of a staff report. All kinds of documents can be attached to a staff report. Sometimes, as in our example, it is the key strategy or program that staff are hoping Council will adopt. If we go back up to our recommendations section, we see that the first recommendation is that Council adopt Attachment 1, which is the new Toronto Festival Strategy. The second attachment is extra information provided by staff about how the new strategy was developed. 

Staff often provide extra context or information as attachments in staff reports. This helps keep the main body of the staff report short and clear for Council and the public to read.
